# EXP-0004 — Recognition Study

**Status:** Scaffolded. Judge = Pedro (n=1, blind); awaits the EXP-0003 Claude arm.
**Started:** 2026-07-18
**Type:** Measurement — provenance-blind classification of AI-generated vs human-authored.
**Consumes:** EXP-0003 corpus (sealed).
**Feeds:** EXP-0005 (the free-text tell-corpus and the discriminative labels).
**Governed by:** [Research Program](../../research/PROGRAM.md), Law of Judge Independence.

## Purpose

Produce the program's first real number: **can a provenance-blind observer separate
AI-generated interfaces from human-authored ones, and how well, per human baseline class.**
This is the instrument every later claim about "reducing the AI look" is measured against.

It tests two hypotheses directly:

- **H1 (existence)** — recognition above chance overall.
- **H4 (the confound)** — recognition against the **trend-following-human** class specifically.
  If observers cannot tell AI from trend-following humans, the phenomenon is *fashion
  membership*, not *model authorship*. That per-class number is the acid test.

## Task given to each observer

For one blind sample (an EXP-0003 PNG named by opaque sample-id, provenance sealed):

1. **Forced choice:** *made by a human, or generated by AI?*
2. **Confidence:** ordinal (guessing / leaning / fairly sure / certain).
3. **Immediacy:** time to first answer, captured passively.
4. **Free-text tell:** *what made you say that?* — one line, written **after** the choice.

The free-text is collected as *hypotheses*, never as ground truth: the point of the study is
that people recognize **before** they can explain, so their explanation is a lead to test in
EXP-0005, not a finding.

## The judge (Pedro, 2026-07-18)

**Pedro is the human panel** — the sole recognizer of record for this corpus (n=1). The
model-judge arm is **deferred**: with a single human judge available and Claude as the only
generator, there is no calibration to run yet. It re-enters when the panel or generator set
widens.

Two caveats are recorded as first-class limitations, not footnotes:

- **n = 1.** Every result is a single expert's recognition, reported with that scope. It is a
  strong pilot signal, never a population claim; nothing promotes to a validated Atlas phenomenon
  on one judge.
- **Expert-judge confound.** Pedro designed the program and holds strong priors about Claude's
  tells ("the Kit"). This *helps* him find tells but does **not** let him cheat the label,
  because the task is **blind** — he does not know which sample is which. If he genuinely cannot
  separate them blind, that is informative regardless of priors. What it limits is
  *generalization*: a naive observer might do worse. Logged, not waved away.

## Calibration (deferred — for when a model-judge or second human is added)

When a second recognizer exists, compare against Pedro on the same blind samples:

- label-agreement rate (with intervals for the small n);
- rank correlation on confidence and on immediacy;
- agreement per baseline class.

The acceptance criterion for "second judge trustworthy at scale" is stated **before** looking at
results, and re-verified whenever generators or model versions change.

## Sampling and presentation

- Balanced across source classes (AI, and each of the three human classes) and across genres.
- Sample order **randomized per observer**; no two observers see the same order.
- Provenance stays sealed for the observer for the entire session; unsealed only at scoring.

## Metrics

- **Recognition accuracy vs chance, reported per baseline class** (the primary result;
  the trend-following-human number is highlighted for H4).
- Confidence calibration (does stated confidence track correctness?).
- Immediacy (does fast recognition correlate with accuracy — the "milliseconds" claim?).
- Inter-observer agreement (do humans agree on *which* samples read as AI?).
- Human–model agreement (the calibration output).

## Threats to validity

- **Trend-vs-authorship confound** — the study is *designed around* it via the per-class
  breakdown; it is measured, not assumed away.
- **Model shares our priors** — mitigated by making humans the ground truth and gating the
  model behind calibration.
- **Learning / order effects** — an observer who sees many samples learns the tells; order is
  randomized and early vs late accuracy is compared to detect learning.
- **Small n** — panel results carry intervals, not point claims; no promotion to a validated
  Atlas phenomenon without independent human review.
- **Genre imbalance** — accuracy is always read per genre as well as per class, since a
  functional dashboard and an expressive landing page are different recognition problems.

## Logistics — the blind must be real for a self-judging author

Because the judge (Pedro) is also the project author, the blinding has to be operationally
airtight or the result is worthless:

- A party that is **not** the judge prepares the blind set: capture all samples identically,
  assign opaque sample-ids, **shuffle**, and hold the provenance key sealed.
- Pedro judges from the shuffled PNGs alone — no filenames, no order signal, no provenance —
  recording label + confidence + immediacy + one-line tell per sample.
- The key is unsealed only after all judgements are locked, then results are scored.
- Pedro does not view the sealed manifest at any point before locking his answers.

## Not in scope here

No intervention, no "fix," no reduction — this experiment only measures recognition. Reduction
(H5) is EXP-0006, and it uses *this* instrument, blind, as its outcome metric.
