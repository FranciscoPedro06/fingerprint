## Layer 2 — Relational Observations

_Observer-dependent. This layer describes **relationships between elements**, not their quality. Lower epistemic status than Layer 1: a second observer may legitimately record differently. Rules: (a) every entry must anchor to at least one Layer-1 fact or screenshot region; (b) evaluative vocabulary remains forbidden ("good", "bad", "beautiful", "generic", "looks AI"); (c) describe relations and distributions, never verdicts._

### Visual hierarchy
_Which mechanisms order attention (size, luminance, position, color, weight)? How many levels? Is the ordering mechanism consistent across sections? Describe as a chain: what reads first → second → third, and by which mechanism._

### Refinement distribution
_Where is micro-detail concentrated, and where is the surface left plain? Uniform polish vs. concentrated polish? Describe as a distribution across the page, not as a quality score._

### Density rhythm
_How does density alternate across the scroll (e.g., sparse → dense → sparse)? Regular cadence or varied intervals? Anchor to the full-page capture._

### Predictability
_Given one section, how anticipatable is the next? Operationalize: approximate count of distinct section schemas vs. total sections observed. Repetition of one schema = high predictability; schema variation = low. This is a structural count, not a judgment._

### Intentional asymmetry
_Deviations from symmetric/grid-centered defaults that recur consistently across the page (consistency of recurrence is the observable; intent itself is not observable — say "recurs consistently", not "was intended")._

### Design risk
_Choices that depart from the observable conventions of the site's genre. Each entry must name the convention and the deviation: "genre convention X; this interface does Y instead." No valuation of whether the deviation succeeds._

### Perceived signature
_Lowest epistemic tier — explicitly the observer's perception. Could a cropped fragment be attributed to this brand without the logo? Which specific elements would carry the attribution? Phrase as "the observer perceived…". List the elements; do not rate their strength._
