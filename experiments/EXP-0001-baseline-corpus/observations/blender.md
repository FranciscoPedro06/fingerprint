## Layer 1 — Objective Observations

### Typography
- Typeface: **Inter** (sans) — confirmed via computed styles. Second corpus record using Inter (see `linear`).
- H1: 72px, **weight 800**, letter-spacing **normal** (no negative tracking). Body: 18px, weight 400. Nav 14px.
- Hierarchy differentiated by size, heavy weight, and per-section heading colors.
- Case usage: mixed — title case section headings ("Latest News", "Blender Development", "Everything You Need"), sentence case body; one period-terminated heading ("It's about people.").

### Color
- Principal chrome colors: light gray page background, white cards, near-black text, blue interactive (Donate button, links).
- **Per-section heading hues**: green ("Free and Open Source", on dark panel), blue ("Part of the industry"), purple/magenta ("It's about people."), white-on-blue-collage ("The Ecosystem"). Four distinct saturated hues as section identities.
- Background treatment: full-bleed photographic/render hero (lioness artwork); alternating white and tinted/dark/collage panels down the page.
- Contrast character: dark-on-light for the portal body; light-on-dark hero and statement panels.

### Depth & effects
- Gradients: within imagery and event graphics (BCON sticker-collage banner); not on page chrome.
- Shadows: subtle on cards and thumbnails.
- Blur/glassmorphism: not observed.
- Glow: not observed.

### Shape & borders
- Corner radius: 6px buttons (computed); small-medium on cards and thumbnails.
- Border usage: minimal; card separation via white-on-gray contrast.

### Space & density
- Whitespace: hero is a full viewport; after it, card rows stack with modest gaps — **no near-empty bands**.
- Visual density: **dense** — full page contains 2-col news row, 4-col development row, 4-col studio row, 4 statement panels, 4-col community photo row, plus footer content; ~20+ cards with thumbnails and body text.

### Layout & components
- Layout: classic portal — full-bleed hero + stacked card rows + full-width statement panels.
- Cards: dominant pattern — image-thumbnail + heading + body-text cards in rows of 2 and 4.
- Component patterns: top nav (8 items + blue Donate), light/dark rectangular hero buttons, news cards, event banners with collage graphics, logo wall (Khronos, Linux Foundation, OIN, ASWF), community photo cards, statement panels with colored display headings.
- Navigation: single top bar.

### Motion
- None blocking; page renders fully in early frame.

## Layer 2 — Relational Observations

_Observer-dependent; anchored to Layer-1 facts and screenshots `01`–`02`._

### Visual hierarchy
Photographic imagery is the dominant mass (hero render, thumbnails); text order is carried by size + weight 800 with hue marking section identity — **each major statement section is announced by a different saturated heading color** (anchor: Layer-1 color observations). Reading chain in hero: lioness image → white H1 over it → buttons. In the portal body, thumbnails read before headings.

### Refinement distribution
Rendering refinement is concentrated in the artwork the community produces (hero render, film stills, event graphics); the page chrome around it is utilitarian (plain cards, default-weight body text, minimal borders). Same shell/content split as `figma`, with community renders in place of user design work.

### Density rhythm
After the hero, density is high and even: card row follows card row with only statement panels as breathers. No sparse bands. Rhythm is that of a news portal (continuous feed), not a staged product narrative.

### Predictability
Approximately **3 section schemas**: (a) full-bleed hero; (b) card row; (c) full-width statement panel. The card-row schema repeats many times; the statement panels vary by hue but not structure. Structure is highly anticipatable; variation is carried by content imagery and heading hue.

### Intentional asymmetry
Minimal: the layout is grid-bound and rectilinear. The deviating elements are content-internal (sticker-collage event banner, off-angle photography). Least asymmetric record so far alongside `obsidian` (comparative observation).

### Design risk
- Corpus-genre convention (professional/dev tools observed so far): restrained single accent. Here **four saturated section hues** on one page.
- Genre convention: product front page as staged narrative. Here the front page is a **dense news portal** where product marketing shares space with community news.
- Corpus convention so far: negative display tracking. Here tracking is normal, compensated by weight 800.

### Perceived signature
The observer perceived attribution-carrying elements in: full-bleed community-made 3D renders under white w800 Inter headlines; the per-section hue shifts; portal density with real community photography. The observer perceived that the hero (render + heavy white headline + version number) would be attributable within the 3D-software genre without the logo.
