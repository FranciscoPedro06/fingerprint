## Layer 1 — Objective Observations

### Typography
- **Single custom typeface: "te-20"** at **weight 100** (ultra-light) for all interface text — computed. Display lettering inside editorial artwork is a separate ultra-condensed heavy face (image content).
- All interface text **lowercase** ("teenage engineering", "products", "visit store", "cart & checkout").
- Spec-sheet register: model numbers with en-dashes ("EP–133", "EP–40") in tabular rows with orange version numerals ("2.5", "1.5").
- A column of **Japanese microcopy** sits in the header as part of the brand voice.

### Color
- White header field; black text; **orange as the single accent** (BUSY sign, version numbers, "UPDATE" tab, product keys); page body sections are near-black photography fields; product photography is black/white/silver hardware with orange details.
- Contrast character: two worlds — white editorial top, black studio-photography bands below.

### Depth & effects
- No UI effects; all depth is photographic (studio lighting on hardware, vinyl records, devices).

### Shape & borders
- No radii, no cards; hairline rules in spec tables; the interface is typographic.

### Space & density
- Header: icon-driven nav (pictograms: fan, cabinet, square, tools) each with a stack of lowercase text links — compact and information-dense.
- Body: one product per full-width dark band (extremely sparse), followed by a 3-up accessory row at bottom.

### Layout & components
- Layout: dense utilitarian header → editorial comic hero ("DAILY LIFE OF MR. UPDATE", black-and-white ink illustration with orange accents) → full-viewport product photography bands (APC-2 stand, vinyl, K.O. II) each with a tiny lowercase caption in a corner → accessory cards.
- Components: pictogram nav clusters, spec-version table, "buy now" lowercase links.
- Navigation: header pictogram clusters only.

### Motion
- None blocking.

## Layer 2 — Relational Observations

_Observer-dependent; anchored to Layer-1 facts and screenshots `01`–`02`._

### Visual hierarchy
Two opposed registers alternate: an information-dense engineering register (ultra-light lowercase type, spec tables, pictograms) and full-bleed theatrical photography where a single product owns a viewport (anchor: layout observations). In the photo bands, hierarchy is one object per beat; captions are nearly invisible (light gray 17px in corners) — the object outranks its own label by orders of magnitude.

### Refinement distribution
Bimodal: extreme refinement in product photography and in the hardware's own industrial design (which the site treats as the content); the interface around it is deliberately under-designed (weight-100 lowercase text, no components). The hero adds a third register: hand-drawn editorial comic.

### Density rhythm
Dense header → mid-density illustrated hero → **viewport-sized sparse beats** (one product each, black fields) → compact accessory row. The swing between header density and product-band emptiness is the largest in the corpus.

### Predictability
Product-band schema repeats; but the hero swaps content type entirely (comic narrative in a store). Register mixing (spec-sheet + comic + studio photography) makes page-level prediction low while band-level prediction is high.

### Intentional asymmetry
Corner-anchored micro-captions on huge empty photography fields; header clusters of unequal width; illustration composition asymmetric. Recurs as a system of small text anchored to large masses.

### Design risk
- Hardware-commerce convention: product name + price + CTA prominently near imagery. Here captions are **nearly illegible by size and placement**, prices absent from bands.
- Convention: brand voice in title case with bold weights. Here **weight-100 lowercase everything**, including the wordmark.
- Convention: consistent register. Here spec-sheet austerity is interleaved with a comic strip — a deliberate register clash.

### Perceived signature
The observer perceived attribution-carrying elements in: weight-100 lowercase te-20 text; en-dashed model numbering with orange numerals; pictogram nav; one-product-per-void photography; the Japanese microcopy column. The observer perceived a cropped spec table alone ("EP–133 … 2.5" in orange) as attributable.
