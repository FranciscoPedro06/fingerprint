## Layer 1 — Objective Observations

### Typography
- Typefaces (computed): **Bradford** (serif — body and headlines, 17px w400, letter-spacing **+0.09px** positive) and **Labil** (sans — labels/tags, 13px w500). Custom sans wordmark.
- Headlines: serif, sentence case, modest sizes (~24–28px) — close to body scale; several headlines are quotations with quotation marks.
- Metadata: relative timestamps ("3 days ago") in small gray; tag labels in small sans.
- Case usage: sentence case throughout; all-caps only in the beige event strip ("NICER TUESDAYS", "ONES TO WATCH").

### Color
- Chrome: white/light-gray field, near-black text (`rgb(20,20,20)`, `rgb(43,43,43)`). **No chrome accent color observed** (the yellow button belongs to the cookie dialog).
- Chromatic content: the article imagery — brand-campaign collages (Tinder reds/magentas), posters, photography, generative art — carries all saturation.
- Background treatment: flat light field; one beige utility strip.
- Contrast character: dark-on-light; high-chroma imagery on quiet chrome.

### Depth & effects
- Gradients/shadows/blur/glow: none observed in chrome.

### Shape & borders
- Corner radius: images sharp; small pill radius only on tag chips and feed cards.
- Border usage: minimal hairlines around feed strip and chips.

### Space & density
- Whitespace: uniform gutters; a wide margin band around the lead story; no empty viewport bands.
- Visual density: dense — lead mega-tile + ~15 article tiles in the capture, plus horizontal feed strip ("The Nice Feed") with 6 compact items.

### Layout & components
- Layout: centered masthead; horizontal news-feed strip; **lead story as an oversized image collage** (grid of ~14 campaign frames) with headline and standfirst below-left; then a 3-column masonry grid with variable image heights.
- Cards: article units — image + serif headline + timestamp + tag chips.
- Component patterns: hamburger menu (single icon nav), feed strip with refresh timestamp ("Refreshed 10h ago"), tag chip system ("Work", "Fashion", "Graphic Design"), video-play tile, speech-bubble illustration tile.
- Navigation: masthead + hamburger only; sections exposed via tags rather than a nav bar.

### Motion
- Not observed in captures.

## Layer 2 — Relational Observations

_Observer-dependent; anchored to Layer-1 facts and screenshots `01`–`02`._

### Visual hierarchy
Ordering is carried by **image area, not typography**: the lead story occupies ~10× the tile area of any other story while its headline stays at feed scale (anchor: layout observations). After the lead, the masonry flattens hierarchy — headlines are uniform in size, and attention order follows image chroma/contrast. Type is a quiet, constant voice; scale decisions live in the imagery.

### Refinement distribution
Chrome refinement is deliberately minimal and uniform (hairlines, chips, timestamps); all visual richness belongs to the curated artwork. Same delegation pattern as `figma`, `blender`, `the-pudding`: the page is a neutral vitrine.

### Density rhythm
Feed strip (compact) → lead mega-tile (single large beat) → constant masonry rhythm with column heights varying by image aspect. One large opening beat followed by an even pulse; no sparse bands.

### Predictability
**Two section schemas** (lead package; masonry tile), high structural repetition. As with `the-pudding`, surface unpredictability is high (every artwork differs) while structure is fixed.

### Intentional asymmetry
Masonry columns end at uneven heights; the lead collage is an irregular grid of unequal frames. Chrome itself is symmetric (centered masthead); asymmetry enters through content geometry.

### Design risk
- Genre convention: design-press sites perform design ambition in their own chrome (display type, color systems). Here the chrome is **near-voiceless** — serif at body scale, no accent, no display typography.
- Genre convention: digital-native press uses sans body. Here the primary voice is a **serif (Bradford)** with slightly positive tracking.
- Genre convention: persistent nav bars. Here navigation is reduced to a hamburger and tag chips.

### Perceived signature
The observer perceived attribution-carrying elements in: the quiet Bradford serif voice at uniform scale; the tag-chip system; the oversized-lead/flat-masonry structure. The observer also perceived that **isolated chrome fragments might not be attributable** — the identity appears to reside in curation (what is shown) more than in chrome (how it is framed). Recorded as a perception, lowest tier.
