## Layer 1 — Objective Observations

### Typography
- **Four-register system** (computed): **Ndot-Regular** (dot-matrix face — wordmark "NOTHING(R)", product names "ear (3a)", system labels), **NType82-Regular** (custom sans, w100 samples), **LatteraMonoLL** (mono), and **Arial bold** for utility buttons ("DISCOVER").
- Marketing line set in a bookish serif register ("Capture what you hear with Audio Snapshot + call recording").
- Case usage: dot-matrix in caps/parenthesized register — "(R)", "ear (3a)" — a machine-readout convention; buttons all-caps Arial 16px w700.

### Color
- Hero: **saturated yellow photographic backdrop** with a faint dot-grid overlay; neutral gray page `rgb(245,245,245)`; black text/buttons; product pop color (pink earbud case).
- Contrast character: fashion photography carries chroma; UI panels are neutral glass/gray.

### Depth & effects
- **Glassmorphism present as core language**: frosted translucent rounded panels (header bar, product card) floating over photography — first corpus record using blur-glass as a primary component treatment.
- No gradients in chrome; photographic depth otherwise.

### Shape & borders
- Corner radius: medium (8px computed on button; larger on glass panels).
- Border usage: none visible; separation via frosted panels.

### Space & density
- Hero: full-bleed photograph + two floating panels (header pill; product card with name, serif line, thumbnail, black button). Sparse UI over rich image.

### Layout & components
- Layout: full-bleed photographic bands with floating glass cards (from hero and archived full page).
- Components: frosted header bar with hamburger + dot-matrix wordmark; product card (dot-matrix name + serif copy + thumbnail + rectangular black CTA); geo modal (on us. domain) using the same language: dot-matrix labels + condensed serif place names.

## Layer 2 — Relational Observations

_Observer-dependent; anchored to Layer-1 facts and screenshot `01` (+ `00` modal)._

### Visual hierarchy
Photography owns the surface; UI floats above it in glass islands (anchor: glassmorphism observation). Within an island the chain is fixed: dot-matrix label → serif benefit line → black CTA. The dot-matrix register marks "the machine speaking" (product names, system labels) while serif marks "the brand speaking" — a two-voice system doing hierarchy by register, as `nytimes` does with serif/sans.

### Refinement distribution
Bimodal like `teenage-engineering`: fashion-grade photography and the hardware's own transparent industrial design carry the richness; UI panels are few and standardized. The dot-grid overlay stitches the photography to the dot-matrix type — a chrome-to-content echo not observed elsewhere in the corpus.

### Density rhythm
Band-per-product with floating cards (from archived full page); sparse UI on dense imagery per beat.

### Predictability
Card schema repeats per product band; the register system is strict. Structure comparable to `apple`'s shelf but with photography-first beats and glass overlays.

### Intentional asymmetry
Floating cards anchor to lower-left/center over off-center photographic subjects; the composition language is editorial rather than centered-symmetric.

### Design risk
- Hardware-commerce convention: neutral studio product shots on white. Here **fashion photography with human subjects** fronts the store.
- Convention: single brand typeface. Here **four registers including Arial** — the deliberately mundane utility font inside a heavily art-directed system.
- Convention: display type for product names. Here product names render in a **dot-matrix machine face** with parenthesized suffixes.

### Perceived signature
The observer perceived attribution-carrying elements in: the Ndot dot-matrix register with parenthesized naming ("ear (3a)"); frosted glass islands over saturated photography; the dot-grid overlay echo. The observer perceived the dot-matrix register alone as instantly attributable within consumer hardware.
