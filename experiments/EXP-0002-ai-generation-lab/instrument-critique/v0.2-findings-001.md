# Instrument Critique — v0.2 against interface-001

> Purpose: evaluate the observation instrument, not the interface. Findings feed v0.3.
> Method: after applying v0.2 exhaustively to one generated SaaS landing page
> (`observations/interface-001.md`), review where the instrument succeeded, failed,
> strained, duplicated itself, or leaned on the observer.

This is iteration 001 of the lab. Under the project principle
*"never scale an instrument that is still learning to observe,"* the instrument is not
changed here — only diagnosed. Changes are proposed for v0.3 and await steward ratification.

---

## 1. What the instrument captured well

- **Typographic facts.** Layer 1 typography was fully fillable from DOM: family, size,
  weight, tracking, per-role color. The `−0.03em` tracking and weight-800 display were
  captured cleanly, and are directly comparable to EXP-0001 SaaS records.
- **Palette structure.** The colour sweep ("distinct colours used") produced a hard,
  reproducible number (≈6 meaningful values) rather than an impression. This is the
  instrument's strongest objective move and should be kept and formalized.
- **Effect inventory.** Gradient count (7), shadow specs, blur presence — all objective
  and reproducible via DOM.
- **Predictability.** The schema-count operationalization worked and produced a clear,
  low number (≈2 schemas), comparable across corpus and lab.
- **Perceived signature.** Even at the lowest epistemic tier, the field earned its place:
  it surfaced the single most important observation of this sample — that the fragment is
  attributable to a *category*, not a *brand*. No Layer-1 field could have surfaced that.

## 2. What stayed invisible to the instrument

These were true of the artifact but have **no field** in v0.2:

- **Correctness / legibility.** A 1.07:1 contrast button (illegible) is invisible to the
  instrument. "Refinement distribution" measures *where polish is concentrated*, not
  *whether it is correct*. An interface can be uniformly "refined" and simultaneously
  broken. v0.2 cannot tell the difference.
- **Convergence to a reference distribution.** The central finding — "every measured
  dimension sits at the centre of its genre convention" — is not a field. The instrument
  measures each interface in isolation; it has no built-in notion of *distance from a
  genre centroid*. "Design risk" gestures at this ("deviation from convention") but only
  as prose, per-item, without a baseline to measure against.
- **Provenance texture.** Emoji-as-icons, gradient-clipped headline word, framework-default
  indigo (`#6366f1`), Google-Fonts-Inter — these read to the observer as *markers of a
  production method* (template / generator defaults). v0.2 records each as a neutral fact
  but has no field for "this element pattern is characteristic of a production lineage."
- **Copy / language register.** "Build better habits, one day at a time", "Simple,
  transparent pricing", "Made with care." The instrument observes typography but is silent
  on text content, tone, and cliché — plausibly a strong signal of the phenomenon.
- **Motion.** Declared hover transitions (lift + shadow, 0.2s) were recorded only in prose
  under an optional field. Static capture cannot see them; the instrument has no defined
  procedure for motion.
- **Interaction states.** Hover, focus, active, disabled — none are in scope. The
  contrast bug only exists because a *state* rule (`.nav-links a`) collided with a
  component rule. State-space is invisible.
- **Spacing system.** The page uses a consistent spacing scale (8/12/24/28/88/96px). The
  instrument notes "generous whitespace" but never captures the *scale* itself, which is
  one of the most method-revealing facts (design-token defaults).

## 3. What was hard to record

- **Viewport dependence.** Size-dependent DOM values changed with the pane width (mobile
  breakpoint at 730px vs desktop screenshots at 1280px). v0.2 says "Viewport: 1280×800"
  but gives no procedure for reconciling DOM extraction viewport with screenshot viewport.
  This produced a real reconciliation cost and a caveat in the record.
- **"Distinct colours" boundary.** Do transparents count? Do a colour and its gradient
  partner count as one or two? The sweep is reproducible only if the counting rule is
  specified; v0.2 leaves it to the observer.
- **Gradient count semantics.** 7 gradients — but 3 are near-identical icon tiles. Is that
  7 uses or 1 pattern used 3×? The instrument does not say whether to count *instances* or
  *distinct treatments*.
- **Single-artifact "Design risk".** The field asks for deviation "from the genre's
  conventions," but the genre baseline lives in the observer's head (or in EXP-0001).
  Recording "no deviation" required importing the EXP-0001 SaaS records by memory —
  undocumented coupling between a lab record and a corpus.

## 4. What seems redundant

- **"Refinement distribution" vs "Density rhythm"** overlapped heavily for this artifact:
  both ended up describing "everything is uniform." When an interface is homogeneous, the
  two fields collapse into the same sentence. They separate only for heterogeneous pages
  (EXP-0001 teenage-engineering). Consider merging into one "uniformity vs concentration"
  axis measured once, with sub-notes for refinement and for density.
- **"Intentional asymmetry"** produced "effectively none" — a near-empty field for this
  and for several EXP-0001 records (obsidian, blender, apple). As a standalone required
  field it is often null. Consider folding it into a single "composition" field alongside
  hierarchy.

## 5. What seems too subjective

- **"Perceived signature"** remains valuable but is entirely observer-anchored. For this
  sample it carried the key insight, yet a second observer could plausibly write the
  opposite. It needs a *procedure* (below), not removal.
- **"Design risk"** mixes an objective part (name the convention, name the deviation) with
  an implicit valuation (is the deviation meaningful?). The objective part is good; the
  word "risk" invites judgement. Rename to "convention deviation" and require the baseline
  to be cited, not remembered.
- **"Refinement"** is a loaded word; it implies quality. For a page with an illegible
  button, calling it "uniformly refined" is almost misleading. Rename to a neutral term
  ("treatment density" / "detail distribution").

## 6. Proposed changes for v0.3

**New Layer-1 fields (objective, DOM-derivable):**
1. **Spacing scale** — list the distinct spacing values used (padding/margin/gap sweep).
2. **Radius scale** — already partially swept; make it a formal field (values + count).
3. **Contrast/legibility measures** — for each text-on-background pair in nav, hero,
   buttons, body: computed WCAG ratio. Objective, reproducible, currently invisible.
4. **External dependencies** — fonts/scripts/CDNs loaded (provenance-relevant, objective).
5. **Copy inventory** — verbatim of headline, subhead, CTA labels, section titles. Text is
   data; record it before interpreting it.

**New Layer-2 fields (relational, procedural):**
6. **Distance-from-genre-centroid** — for each measured dimension, mark
   `at-centre / off-centre / far` *relative to a named reference set* (e.g. EXP-0001 SaaS
   stratum). Requires the baseline to be cited. This operationalizes what "design risk"
   only gestured at, and directly targets the convergence phenomenon.
7. **Signature locus** — split "perceived signature" into *chrome-signature* vs
   *content-signature*, and add **attribution target**: does a cropped fragment read as
   `brand / category / production-method / none`? (interface-001 = category; most EXP-0001
   = brand.) This single field may be the most phenomenon-relevant addition.

**Merges / renames:**
8. Merge "refinement distribution" + "density rhythm" → **"uniformity vs concentration"**
   (one axis, two sub-notes).
9. Fold "intentional asymmetry" into **"composition"** with visual hierarchy.
10. Rename "design risk" → **"convention deviation"** (baseline citation required);
    rename "refinement" → **"detail distribution"** (neutral).

**Procedures to add:**
11. **Viewport procedure** — fix DOM-extraction viewport to match the screenshot viewport,
    or record both states explicitly. Default: 1280 desktop for both.
12. **Counting rules** — specify instance-vs-distinct-treatment for colours, gradients,
    radii. Default: report both (`N instances / M distinct`).
13. **Motion procedure** — at minimum, extract declared CSS transitions/animations from
    source and list them; note that static capture cannot verify runtime motion.
14. **Correctness note** — a short objective defects section (contrast, overflow, broken
    states) separate from aesthetic observation, so "detail distribution" is never confused
    with "correctness."

## 7. Meta-observation (candidate project-level insight, 🟡)

🟡 "The instrument was built (EXP-0001) on interfaces with strong brand identities, where
its job was to describe *what makes each distinct*. Pointed at a generated interface, its
most important finding was the near-*absence* of distinguishing signal — attribution to a
category, not a brand. This suggests the phenomenon 'AI look' may be better operationalized
as **low distance from a genre centroid + attribution-to-category**, than as any positive
list of traits (Inter, indigo, gradients). The v0.3 additions (#6, #7) are designed to
measure that directly. To be tested against further samples before being treated as more
than a hypothesis."

## 8. Instrument maturity status

- New **required** fields proposed this iteration: 5 Layer-1, 2 Layer-2, plus merges and
  procedures. This is a large delta → **instrument is NOT stable**; corpus-scale
  collection remains deferred.
- Exit criterion reminder: two consecutive iterations with *no new required fields*.
  Iteration 001 does not qualify. Recommend building v0.3, then running iteration 002 on a
  second generated interface (ideally from an **isolated generator** to begin satisfying
  the independence requirement).
