# Atlas

The Atlas is the catalog of what has been observed to recur: **phenomena** (mechanisms of
recognition) and **fingerprints** (concrete instances through which a phenomenon appears).

## Two divisions by status

- **Experimental.** Hypotheses drawn from observation. Each entry states the observation
  that raised it, the evidence available, and what would refute it. Nothing here is
  treated as established.
- **Validated.** Entries that have survived independent human review under the
  [Law of Judge Independence](../methodology/laws-and-principles.md). Promotion from
  experimental to validated is a human decision, never automatic.

## Phenomenon vs fingerprint

A **phenomenon** is durable ("recognition by proximity to genre convention"). A
**fingerprint** is a perishable instance of it (a specific color, typeface, or effect in
wide use at some time). They are catalogued separately so that the disappearance of a
fingerprint never invalidates the phenomenon it once illustrated.

## Entry requirements

Every entry MUST:

- carry a status (experimental | validated);
- cite the observation records ([corpus](../corpus/)) that support it, in Observation
  Language terms (evidence → relations → hypotheses → attribution);
- state its refutation condition;
- preserve competing explanations where they exist.
