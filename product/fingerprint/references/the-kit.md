# The Kit — your own fingerprint as a generator

Every centroid catalogued elsewhere is a *style* (dark-luxury, editorial-beige, bold-retro).
This file is different and more important: it names **the genre-independent kit of atoms that
you, the model, assemble no matter what you're asked to build.** It is your *own* fingerprint —
the thing that makes your output recognizable as yours across completely different briefs.

The evidence is direct. Across four outputs for four different briefs — a dark-luxury barbershop
(*Ofício*), a bold-retro barbershop (*Régua*), a ledger barbershop (*Corvo*), and a precision
dental clinic (*Meridiano*) — the *styles* looked unrelated, but the **same atoms recurred in
almost every one:**

| Kit atom | Ofício | Régua | Corvo | Meridiano |
|---|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|
| `·`-separated tracked-caps eyebrow | ● | ● | ● | ● |
| Giant headline, **one word in the accent**, ending in a period | ● | ● | ● | ● |
| Exactly **one** accent color | ● | ● | ● | ● |
| Warm-neutral/cream **or** charcoal background (often a faint grid/dot texture) | ● | ● | ● | ● |
| Two-button hero: **filled + bordered/underlined**, with `→`/`↓` | ● | ● | ● | ● |
| A **plate on the right** of the hero (image or widget) with a mono caption | ● | ● | ● | ● |
| **Mono** for every micro-label | ● | ● | ● | ● |
| **"DESDE 20XX"** and/or an index/credential number (`Nº`, `FÓLIO`, `01`, `CRO-SP`) | ● | ● | ● | ● |
| **Stat triplet**: three numbers + mono labels + dividers | ● | ○ | ○ | ● |
| Reflex trust-copy: "sem surpresa", "preço fechado", "precisão", "40 min" | ● | ● | ○ | ● |

The smoking gun is **self-citation**: the phrase **"LUZ DE TUNGSTÉNIO"** appears verbatim in
*two* unrelated outputs; **"40 min" / "40'"**, **"DESDE 20XX"**, and **"sem surpresa"** recur
across briefs that share nothing. There was no real brand in any of them, so the placeholder got
filled with **the same paint every time.** That is the Kit.

---

## The rule

**The Kit is your default signature. Assembling it is producing the AI look, regardless of style,
theme, or genre.** Restyling the Kit (serif to condensed, gold to green, dark to cream) does not
escape it. Narrating a concept over it (Corvo's "ledger") does not escape it. Only *not assembling
it* does.

The countable gate lives in the `SKILL.md` audit: **two atoms or fewer.** This file is the atom
list that gate counts against. Each atom you do keep must survive the same test as any other
decision, a verifiable origin in this brand, this genre, this content, and never "it looks clean
or technical or premium." Reaching for any of them is a warning, not a solution.

## What to do instead — this is not a new forbidden list, it's a floor

Do **not** respond by inventing an anti-Kit that becomes its own Kit (no-headline, no-color,
all-brutalist, all-monospace). That is just the next fingerprint — and it is usually *ugly*, which
fails the real bar. **The goal is a beautiful interface that doesn't look AI-made, not a bare one.**
A page can have zero Kit atoms and be cold, cramped, and unlovable — that is a failure, not a pass.
Ornament, warmth, richness, and polish are all allowed; only the AI *reflexes* are not. Instead:

1. **Let the structural idea set the composition** (see [`authored-vs-ai.md`](authored-vs-ai.md)),
   so the page's *shape* isn't the hero-eyebrow-plate skeleton the Kit lives in. If the shape is
   forced to be a booking ledger, a real odontogram, a price board, a queue — the Kit atoms have
   nowhere to attach.
2. **Source type, color, and copy from outside your defaults.** The Kit's fonts, its one-accent
   habit, its "DESDE"/"sem surpresa"/"precisão" phrases are *your* memory talking. Pull from the
   real brand, or from plain system defaults used with conviction — not from the paint you reused
   last time.
3. **Vary the count, not just the coat.** A genuinely different output should differ in *which
   components exist at all* — not in the color of the same components. Ofício and Meridiano have
   the same component inventory in different colors; that is the failure.

## The honest limit

You cannot fully see your own Kit — it feels neutral to you, the way your handwriting does. That
is why this list is concrete and countable rather than a matter of taste: you can *check* whether
you used the `·`-caps eyebrow far more reliably than you can *feel* whether you were generic. And
even this is not enough on its own — a truly independent check (a separate reviewer, an eval that
didn't build the page) is the only reliable judge, because the maker grades on a curve. Use this
list as the floor; do not mistake passing it for authorship.
